Name: The Global Polio Eradication Initiative
Kind: The Global Polio Eradication Initiative is a public-private partnership led by national governments and spearheaded by the World Health Organization (WHO), Rotary International, the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), and the United Nations Children’s Fund (UNICEF).
Location: Geneva, Switzerland.
History: Since 1988, more than 2.5 billion children have been immunized against polio thanks to the unprecedented cooperation of more than 200 countries and 20 million volunteers, backed by an international investment of over US$ 8 billion. By 2006, only four countries remained that had never stopped polio transmission, and annual case numbers had decreased by over 99%.  In 2008, the World Health Assembly called for a new strategy to eradicate polio from the remaining affected countries and finally rid the world of polio. The Global Polio Eradication Initiative launched an intensified eradication effort involving the wide-scale use of new tools and tactics in each country, with renewed commitment by their leaders and donors.

Name: The International Monetary Fund
Kind: International economic organization
Location: Washington, D.C.
History: The IMF was created on July 22, 1944 at the Bretton Woods Conference with the intention of rebuilding the global economic system after WWII. The IMF was formally organized on December 27, 1945 when 29 countries signed the Articles of Agreement. The IMF functions like a bank and makes sure that borrowing states can pay their debts on time. The IMF also offers member nations information on economic policies of all member nations, financial support in times of economic difficulties, and increased opportunities for trade and investment. The IMF is one of the key organizations of the international economic system. Currently, the IMF has 188 member nations and its largest borrowers are Greece, Portugal, Ireland, Romania, and Ukraine.
